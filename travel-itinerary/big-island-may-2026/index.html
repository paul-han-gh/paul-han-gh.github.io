Departure

Fly into Kona International

Book the earliest available flight into Ellison Onizuka Kona International Airport (KOA). Getting on island early is critical — you want maximum daylight before the summit drive.

Morning ~8:30 AM
First Stop

Kona Coffee Farm Tour

Right off the plane, head to the slopes of Hualalai where the world-famous Kona coffee belt begins. A guided farm tour covers the full growing and milling process — end with a tasting flight before hitting the road.

Several farms along Mamalahoa Highway offer walk-in tours. Greenwell Farms and Mountain Thunder are reliable options that don't require advance booking.
Midday 12 PM – 4 PM
To Be Decided

Afternoon Block — Open

This window is unplanned. Options in the area include Puuhonua o Honaunau National Historical Park (the Place of Refuge), a snorkel at Kealakekua Bay, or a slow lunch in Kailua-Kona town before the drive north to Saddle Road.

~5:00 PM 2 hrs before sunset
Pre-Summit Prep

Fill Up — Last Gas Before the Mountain

Fill the tank completely at the last gas station before Saddle Road. Once you pass Hilo or Waimea and head toward the access road, options disappear. Load up on water and snacks at the same stop.

Do not skip this. There are zero gas stations or food services on Mauna Kea Access Road or at the summit.
~5:30 PM Summit by sunset
Main Event

Mauna Kea Summit — Sunset Over the Clouds

Drive the Mauna Kea Access Road to the 13,796-ft summit. The view from above the cloud layer as the sun sets is one of the rarest sights on Earth. Arrive 30–45 minutes before sunset to find your spot. Sunset is around 7:13 PM in mid-May.

Heavy winter wear required — no exceptions. Temperatures at the summit drop below freezing after sundown. Everyone must bring an insulated jacket, gloves, and a warm hat. The wind chill at this altitude is severe.
After Dark ~8:00 PM+
Night Sky

Stargazing — Visitor Information Station

After the summit, drive down to the Maunakea Visitor Information Station at 9,200 ft — the nearest parking lot on the descent. The VIS hosts free nightly stargazing programs with public telescopes. At this elevation, far above the marine layer, the sky is extraordinarily dark and clear.

Free, no reservation required. Programs run nightly from around 6 PM, weather permitting. The temperature here is far more manageable than the summit.
Late Night ~10:00 PM+
Overnight

Drive to Hawi — Settle In

From the Visitor Station it's roughly 45 minutes north to Hawi. Check into Kimia's father's 3-bedroom property — your complimentary base for the night. Rest well. If weather cooperates, Day 2 starts early.

Early AM Clear weather only
Morning Hike

Kohala Waterfall Slide Hike

If the overnight brings clear skies, head out for a hike near Hawi featuring natural water plumes and rock slides. The Kohala Ditch Trail system winds through dense jungle leading to exactly this — sliding pools carved through volcanic rock by centuries of flow.

Clear weather only. Flash flooding is a real risk in Kohala's narrow gulches. If there was any rain overnight or in the forecast, skip this and save it for next time.
Post-Hike Midday
Departure

Four Travelers Head to KOA

After the hike, four members of the group drive south to Kona International Airport. Allow 1–1.5 hours from Hawi to the airport, plus time to return the 4WD rental. Coordinate the vehicle dropoff the night before to avoid a rushed morning.

Return the rental car before check-in. The Hawi-to-Kona drive on Akoni Pule Highway is scenic but not fast — leave with buffer time.

Joe & Peachy — One Year of Marriage

Joe and Peachy stay behind to mark their first anniversary on the island. The rest of the itinerary is theirs. Waipio Valley lookout, a quiet beach along the Kohala Coast, or simply nothing — they've earned the day.

Before You Go

Practical details on accommodation, the rental vehicle, and what to pack for the summit.

Accommodation

Location: Hawi, North Kohala

Property: Kimia's father's 3-bedroom home

Cost: Complimentary — no lodging expense. Consider a thoughtful thank-you for the host before arriving.

4WD Rental

Duration: 1 day

Required: 4WD is mandatory above the Mauna Kea VIS. Standard vehicles are prohibited on the summit road.

Cost: Split equally, 6 ways.

What to Pack

Heavy insulated jacket — Summit is below freezing

Gloves + warm hat — Wind chill at 13,796 ft is brutal

Swimwear + water shoes — Waterfall hike on Day 2

Snacks + water — No services on the summit road

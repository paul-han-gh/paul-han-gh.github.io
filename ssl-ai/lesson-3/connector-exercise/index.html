AI Platform Connectors: Practical Exercises

Hands-on walkthroughs for connecting your everyday tools to ChatGPT, Claude, and Gemini — then putting those connections to real use.

ChatGPT Claude Gemini
Before you begin: Each section below starts with setup instructions for connecting tools to that platform, followed by practical exercises you can try immediately. You will need a paid plan on most platforms to access connector features. All exercises use general-purpose apps most people already have accounts for (Google Drive, Outlook, Notion, etc.).

ChatGPT

Apps & Connectors by OpenAI

How to Set Up a Connector in ChatGPT

ChatGPT calls its integrations "Apps" (previously called "Connectors"). They let ChatGPT search, reference, and act on data from third-party services. Available on Plus, Pro, Team, Enterprise, and Edu plans.

Available Connectors Include:

Google Drive Gmail Outlook SharePoint Dropbox OneDrive Slack Microsoft Teams HubSpot GitHub Canva Spotify Booking.com Coursera

Step-by-Step Setup

  1. Open ChatGPT and click your profile icon in the top-right corner, then choose Settings.
  2. In the left sidebar, click Apps (on older interfaces this may still say "Connectors").
  3. Browse the App Directory. Find the service you want to connect (for example, Google Drive) and click Connect.
  4. You will be redirected to that service's sign-in page. Log in with your credentials and approve the permissions ChatGPT is requesting. Read these carefully.
  5. Once connected, return to ChatGPT. Start a new conversation and use an @ mention (type @ then the app name) or click the + button → More to attach the app to your conversation.
  6. Test it: Ask something simple like "Show me my most recent Google Drive files" or "What are my unread emails?" to verify the connection is working.
Tip: Some connectors support "Sync" mode, which pre-indexes your data for faster responses. You can enable this in Settings → Apps for supported connectors like Google Drive.

Exercises

Exercise 1

Weekly Email Digest with Gmail

Use the Gmail connector to have ChatGPT summarize your important emails from the past week and draft replies.

Connector: Gmail

  1. Connect the Gmail app following the setup steps above.
  2. Start a new chat and add Gmail using the + menu or by typing @Gmail.
  3. Use the prompt below to generate a summary.
  4. Review the summary. Pick one email and ask ChatGPT to draft a reply.
  5. Copy the drafted reply into Gmail, review it, and send.
Sample Prompt "@Gmail — Summarize my most important emails from the last 7 days. Group them by topic, highlight any that need a reply, and note any upcoming deadlines mentioned."
Tip: Include the word "Gmail" in your prompt even if you've already attached the app — this helps ChatGPT reliably trigger the connector.

Claude

Integrations & Connectors by Anthropic

How to Set Up a Connector in Claude

Claude uses Integrations (also called "Connectors") built on the open-source Model Context Protocol (MCP). They allow Claude to read from, write to, and take actions in your connected tools. Available on Pro, Max, Team, and Enterprise plans.

Available Connectors Include:

Google Drive Gmail Notion Slack Asana Linear Jira Confluence Figma Canva Zapier Intercom GitHub Custom MCP Servers

Step-by-Step Setup (Web Connectors)

  1. Open claude.ai and click on your profile icon, then go to Settings.
  2. Click Connectors in the left sidebar.
  3. Browse the Connectors Directory. Find the integration you want (for example, Notion) and click Connect.
  4. You'll be taken through an OAuth sign-in flow. Log in to the third-party service and approve the permissions Claude is requesting.
  5. Return to Claude. In any conversation, click the "Search and tools" menu (or the tools icon) to verify your connector appears and is enabled.
  6. Test it: Start a conversation and ask something like "Show me my recent Notion pages" or "List my Asana tasks."

Adding a Custom Connector (Advanced)

  1. In Settings → Connectors, scroll to the bottom and click "Add custom connector."
  2. Enter a name and the remote MCP server URL for your tool.
  3. Optionally, provide OAuth credentials under "Advanced settings."
  4. Click Add. Authenticate when prompted.
Tip: Claude connectors are not just read-only. Many support write actions (creating tasks, sending messages, etc.). Always review Claude's tool-approval requests carefully before clicking "Allow."

Gemini

Extensions & Workspace Apps by Google

How to Set Up Extensions in Gemini

Gemini uses "App Connections" (formerly called "Extensions") to integrate with Google's own services and select third-party tools. The strongest connectors are the native Google Workspace integrations: Gmail, Google Drive, Google Docs, Calendar, Keep, and Tasks. Available on Google AI Pro, AI Ultra, and Workspace plans.

Available Connectors Include:

Gmail Google Drive Google Docs Google Calendar Google Keep Google Tasks Google Maps YouTube Google Flights Google Hotels

Step-by-Step Setup

  1. Open gemini.google.com and sign in with your Google account.
  2. Click the Settings icon (gear icon) or go to Settings from the side menu.
  3. Find the "App Connections" section (may also appear as "Extensions" on some versions).
  4. You will see toggles for each Google service. Turn on the ones you want — for example, switch on Google Workspace to enable Gmail, Drive, and Docs access.
  5. For Workspace accounts: your organization's admin may need to enable Workspace extensions first. Check with your IT department if the toggles are grayed out.
  6. Test it: Return to a Gemini conversation and ask "What are my unread emails?" or "What's on my calendar today?" to verify the connection.
Important: Gemini's extensions work best with Google's own services. Unlike ChatGPT and Claude, Gemini does not yet have a broad directory of third-party connectors in the consumer app — its strength is deep integration with the Google ecosystem.
